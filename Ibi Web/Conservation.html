Efforts Related to MORs globally

Although MORs fall into the "of least concern" by Internation Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN), there are regional and national scale efforts being made all over the world to protect and nurture these magnificent birds in particular localities.

Global Giving Org: Conservation of the Natural Bird of India

Peafowl, the Indian National bird, is protected under Indian Wildlife Act 1972. They are hunted for feathers, fat and meat. The incidents of high mortality in some areas denote poaching. So, the project aims to provide awareness through street theatre/seminar/IEC for local populace. The project also aims to provide counseling for poachers with bank assisted alternate livelihood option for them.

Through this project, the poachers will be identified and rehabilitated with alternate livelihood options. The villages level 'Peacock Protection Force (PPF)' will help protecting the bird, prevent poaching and discourage peacock habitat land conversion, amongst other initiatives.

Read more here: GlobalGiving.Org

Yun Pa Reserve: Conservation of green Peafowl in Vietnam

The green peafowl is the galliform species at most immediate risk in Indochina. The species has dramatically declined, or even disappeared, in most of its historical range in recent decades. The overal goal of this project is to make contributions to understanding the status and distribution, as well as to save one of the most endangered bird species in Vietnam. The team includes young conservationists participating in activities ranging from field surveys to conservation education. Project activities will be focused on providing updated information on the status of Green Peafowl in A Yun Pa nature reserve; enhancing capacity in wildlife monitoring and conservation; and reducing pressures, especially hunting activities by local community members to targeted species.
